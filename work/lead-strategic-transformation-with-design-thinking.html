Lead Strategic Transformation with Design Thinking

UX Strategy User Research Facilitation

Customer demos are part of Salesforce DNA. However, their creation requires substantial knowledge and practice that most individuals acquire over time and experience or through a fairly steep ramp up. I orchestrated a cross-functional effort that required significant stakeholder alignment and buy-in. Using a design sprint as a catalyst, we unified perspectives and ultimately drove convergence toward a shared vision and a cohesive user experience.

The team responsible to create and maintain demo templates grown organically over the years. It quickly became multiple sub-teams, each looking after different industry specific solutions. Their processes diverged, new technologies emerged, user experience and documentation became inconsistent, impacting distribution efficiency and overall scalability. With so many challenges to solve we had to wisely chose our priorities: focus on the end-user experience and team delivery performance.

FigJam whiteboard after 3 days of remote design sprint sessions.

How Might We...

... ensure that the global SE audience has a reliable and consistent experience tailored to their needs, by providing useful, targeted recommendations for demo add-ons based on relevant criteria, while maintaining a high level of content quality and relevance without requiring additional resources for QA, verification, and documentation?

Approach

Design a 3-day version of Design Sprint for remote global teams.

Identify key stakeholders to participate to the sprint. This is critical part of the strategy to eventually operate change management.

Facilitate the design sprint. Bring participants through a series of intense but fun activities.

Design mid-fidelity prototype to restitute the vision created collectively and test it with target audience.

Test and validate the vision, and collect feedback via focus groups.

We delivered a vision and a storyline as a team. We also designed a prototype to convey concepts visually.

Outcomes

  1. NextGen demo experience vision with high level prototype validated with target audience.
  2. Two comprehensive user story.
  3. List of strengths and weaknesses of the solution in regards of the initial challenge.
  4. Team and department alignment towards unified goal.

Key Learnings

  • Aligning divergent perspectives requires structure and shared creation. Working with individuals holding different points of view is inherently challenging. We found that using design thinking helped unify stakeholders by involving everyone in shaping the vision hands-on. It not only aligned perspectives but also generated momentum, boosted morale, and energized the team.
  • Stakeholder buy-in is essential for meaningful transformation. We experimented with multiple UX-driven initiatives, but only when we secured strong stakeholder commitment did we see real changes in behavior and decision-making. Alignment at the top enables traction on the ground.
  • High-level design sprints need clear follow-through. This type of design sprint is valuable for crafting a strong narrative and rough product vision, but it can introduce ambiguity. To avoid misalignment, it’s essential to plan for the next steps—such as detailed design, user validation, and MVP scoping—early in the process.
